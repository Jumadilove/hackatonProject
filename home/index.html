Sights
Horse rides
Horseback riding takes place in Salkyn Tor. The valley offers a beautiful view of the mountains and the forest. Horseback riding or hippotherapy is very beneficial for children and adults.
Tash-Rabat
Caravanserai "Tash-Rabat" is a unique architectural monument of the early Middle Ages, the appearance of which more specifically dates back to the 15th century, and one of the main archaeological sites of Kyrgyzstan, which has become a symbol of Tenir-Too mountain architecture. It is located in the picturesque gorge-canyon Kara-Koyun (Naryn region), 110 km south of the city of Naryn, near the border with China, at an altitude of 3200 meters above sea level.
Son-Kul
Lake Son-Kul is a fabulous high-mountainous lake of the Tien Shan, in the usual translation into Russian means "Last Lake". It is located at an altitude of 3016 meters above sea level, the depth reaches 22 m. This is the largest natural freshwater reservoir in Kyrgyzstan.
Kel-Suu
Lake Kel-Suu is considered to be one of the most beautiful mountain lakes in Kyrgyzstan. It is located in a remote area in the western part of the Kokshaal-Too mountains, not far from the border with China. Just a few years ago it was little known, but today this place attracts tourists not only from Kyrgyzstan, but from all over the world.
Chatyr-Kul
Lake Chatyr-Kul is the third largest lake in Kyrgyzstan: its area is 175 sq. km. km. At the same time, it is relatively shallow - up to 20 m. For most of the year, the lake is ice-bound, which is explained by its location among glaciers. The lake is part of the Karatal-Zhapyryk National Reserve. Various species of animals and birds live on the banks of Chatyr-Kul - snow leopards, marmots, mountain goats and mountain geese.

Naryn
Nestled into the foothills of Naryn-Too, Naryn city is home to around 40,000 people, as well as museums, institutes, and several administrative buildings. The city was once a small fortification on a trade route running from Kashgar to Central Asia, and it didn't actually become a city until the 19th century. Being just slightly over 100 miles from the Chinese border, Naryn is an important trade center and a free economic zone.
